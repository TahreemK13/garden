🌱 On my mind

I didn’t realize how common it is to dismiss philosophy as meaningless or useless. On the surface it may seem abstract, unable to provide obvious tangible contributions to society. But look, just a little deeper, and the truth becomes obvious. Philosophy isn’t about just sitting around and thinking; that sentiment drives me into a veritable rage (a 'valid crashout' as the kids say). Rather it is the field of study that teaches you how to think. No more and no less. Introductory philosophy courses will often warn students that they will start to see the world differently by the culimnation of the class and to consider another course, if the possibility makes them uncomfortable. The idea is that after grappling with enough head spinning reading material, a person starts to shed conventional modes of thinking and opens their mind to new possibilites. Cue up 'A Whole New World' by Aladdin, and read up on the 'Cartesian Method of Doubt' by René Descartes people. There's a perfect Calvin and Hobbes comic to sign off on this rant, but I'm just going to go to bed. March 2026 - My first garden seedling
